# Vehicle Maintenance Guide

## Overview

Maintenance requirements vary significantly between Electric Vehicles (EVs), Gasoline vehicles, and Hybrids. Understanding these differences will help you plan ownership costs accurately.

## Electric Vehicles (EV)

### What EVs Do NOT Require
- Oil changes
- Transmission fluid changes
- Spark plug replacements
- Timing belt service
- Exhaust system maintenance

### EV Routine Service Schedule

| Interval | Service Required |
|----------|-----------------|
| Every 6 months / 7,500 miles | Tire rotation, brake inspection, cabin air filter check |
| Every 12 months / 15,000 miles | Tire rotation, brake fluid check, wiper blade replacement, multi-point inspection |
| Every 2 years / 25,000 miles | Cabin air filter replacement, brake fluid replacement |
| Every 4 years / 50,000 miles | Coolant system flush (battery thermal management) |
| Every 6 years / 75,000 miles | High-voltage battery health diagnostic |

### EV Battery Care Tips
- Avoid charging to 100% daily — keep daily charge between 20% and 80% to extend battery life.
- Use DC fast charging (Level 3) sparingly; regular Level 2 home charging is preferred.
- Avoid leaving the vehicle at 0% charge for extended periods.
- Battery capacity loss of up to 10–15% over 100,000 miles is considered normal.

### EV Estimated Annual Maintenance Cost
**$300–$600/year** (excluding tires and unexpected repairs)

## Gasoline Vehicles

### Gasoline Routine Service Schedule

| Interval | Service Required |
|----------|-----------------|
| Every 5,000–7,500 miles | Oil and oil filter change |
| Every 15,000 miles | Tire rotation, air filter inspection, brake inspection |
| Every 30,000 miles | Air filter replacement, fuel filter check, spark plug inspection |
| Every 45,000 miles | Transmission fluid check, coolant inspection |
| Every 60,000 miles | Spark plug replacement, timing belt inspection, brake fluid flush |
| Every 90,000 miles | Timing belt replacement (if applicable), major tune-up |

### Gasoline Estimated Annual Maintenance Cost
**$700–$1,500/year** (excluding tires and unexpected repairs)

---

## Hybrid Vehicles

Hybrids combine gasoline engine maintenance with a smaller battery system. They require more attention than EVs but less than pure gasoline vehicles.

### Hybrid Routine Service Schedule

| Interval | Service Required |
|----------|-----------------|
| Every 5,000–7,500 miles | Oil and oil filter change (engine oil) |
| Every 10,000 miles | Tire rotation, brake inspection |
| Every 30,000 miles | Air filter replacement, spark plug inspection, hybrid battery check |
| Every 45,000 miles | Transmission fluid inspection, coolant check |
| Every 60,000 miles | Spark plug replacement, brake fluid flush, hybrid system diagnostic |
| Every 100,000 miles | Hybrid battery capacity test |

### Hybrid Brake System Note
Most hybrids use regenerative braking, which significantly reduces brake pad wear. Brake pads typically last **2× longer** than on a conventional gasoline vehicle.

### Hybrid Estimated Annual Maintenance Cost
**$500–$1,000/year** (excluding tires and unexpected repairs)
